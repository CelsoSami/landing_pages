About Me

Celso Sami

Data & Analytics Engineer with 8+ years turning complex data into scalable, business-driven solutions across finance and healthcare.

I started my career as a Civil Engineer, building hospitals and managing infrastructure projects. But what truly fascinated me was the data behind every decision — the numbers that determined which projects moved forward, which resources were allocated, and which outcomes changed lives.

In 2018, while still working in construction, I began developing hospital dashboards and indicators. That intersection of engineering discipline and data storytelling became my calling. Within a few years, I transitioned fully into Data & Analytics — and haven't looked back.

Today I design end-to-end data pipelines, build predictive models, and lead BI ecosystems that serve thousands of users. I've worked in regulated financial cooperatives, large hospital networks, and cooperative health systems — always at the crossroads of technical depth and business impact.

What drives me most is the moment raw data becomes a decision — when a dashboard reveals a pattern no one saw before, when a model prevents a loss, or when a pipeline delivers the right information at the right time.

Common Questions

What drives your work?

The moment raw data becomes a decision. I'm obsessed with the bridge between messy, complex data and clear, actionable insight. Every pipeline I build, every model I train, every dashboard I design is aimed at making someone's decision easier, faster, or better-informed.

What's your experience with Data Engineering?

Deep and end-to-end. I've built pipelines processing 380+ data sources in Databricks, implemented API integrations, orchestrated ETL/ELT workflows, and migrated entire BI ecosystems. I care as much about data quality and governance as about throughput and performance.

What's unique about your background?

I came from Civil Engineering — building hospitals, managing construction projects. That experience gave me a structured, process-oriented mindset and deep understanding of how data impacts real-world operations. I know what it means to work in regulated environments where accuracy isn't optional.

What kind of role are you looking for?

I'm drawn to roles where I can build data solutions that create measurable business impact — whether that's designing pipelines, leading BI strategy, or building ML models. I thrive in environments where technical depth meets real business problems.
